1941 RAF (Central)

RAF aerial photographic surveys. These were carried out all over Great Britain under the name Operation Revue, they were intended to help revise maps, assist town planning and road building after WWII.

1942 Air Raid Precautions

This map shows fire action stations, emergency centres and warden posts around Bexhill during WWII. It was created by Borough Surveyor Harold Percy Storey in March 1942.

1925 Maynard's Chronicle

Hand-drawn map by the then Bexhill's architect and surveyor John Edward Maynard (not to scale).

1795 Yeakell, Gardner & Gream

1778 Yeakell & Gardner

Thomas Yeakell and William Gardner were originally employed by Charles Lennox (3rd Duke of Richmond) to create a map of Sussex. However, only half of the map was ever completed due to lack of funds. When Yeakell died in 1787, Gardner returned to his map of Sussex, working alongside surveyor Thomas Gream, which was completed in 1795, although on a reduced scale.
