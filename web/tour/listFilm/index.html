Filming Locations

A number of films have been made in Bexhill over the last century, the towns period architecture, accessible beaches and surrounding countryside are often prominently featured.

Sherlock Holmes (1912)

The first official Sherlock Holmes films were made in Bexhill; starring Georges Tréville. Of the eight 'two-reelers' filmed, only two survive.

The interiors for the series were shot inside the Kursaal (demolished 1936, now the location of Bexhill Sailing Club). When a move had to be made outdoors, Aukland House in Sidley was one chosen location.1 The Copper Beeches [24:06] The Musgrave Ritual [29:42]

It's a Bet (1935)

Adapted from a novel "Hide and I'll Find You" by Marcus McGill; directed by Alexander Esway; starring Gene Gerrard, Helen Chandler and Judy Kelly.
A young English reporter makes a bet with a wealthy publisher that he can disappear for a month. In his absence the publisher makes much of the mysterious disappearance in an attempt to boost the circulation of his newspaper.

A scene about 15 minutes into the film shows Rollo Briggs jumping from St Leonard's 'New Palace' Pier (dismantled in the early 1950s) and swimming to Galley Hill. Upon reaching the shore, Briggs steals another swimmer's clothes, is chased up the cliff and takes off on a waiting bus. It's a Bet [1:07:34]

Double Confession (1950)

Adapted from a novel "All On a Summer's Day" by John Garden; directed by Ken Annakin; starring Derek Farr, Joan Hopkins and Peter Lorre.
After his wife is murdered, the husband tries to divert suspicion from himself to someone else. Unfortunately, his scheme winds up getting him mixed up with some real murderers.

Various scenes show Bexhill's seafront and Marina, with external shots of the De La Warr Pavilion and one of the Edwardian shelters on East Parade. Double Confession [1:24:10]

The Quiet Woman (1951)

Based on a story by Ruth Adam; directed by John Gilling; starring Derek Bond, Jane Hylton and Dora Bryan.
A young woman's past comes back to haunt her when she attempts to build a new life at an inn.

Although the film is set in Romney Marsh, The Star Inn was used for external shots of the public house. The Quiet Woman [1:07:03]

Other Womens Lives: Talking Shop (1953)

Part of a series of cinema commercials made by Cadbury's using real names and addresses.
Mrs Cowlard of Bexhill-on-Sea is a busy housewife and mother who also runs a pet shop in the town. In the evening she and her husband like nothing more than to relax with a nice cup of Bourn-vita.

The advert shows scenes around Sidley, the Old Town and Bexhill beach. British Film Institute

Piece of Cake (1988)

A six-part television serial produced for ITV; directed by Ian Toynton; starring Neil Dudgeon, George Anton and Boyd Gaines.
The lives of a Royal Air Force fighter squadron during the early days of WWII and the Battle of Britain.

A small segment in episode 5 shows two pilots leaving Cooden Beach Station by taxi. Piece of Cake (Episode 1) [52:35]

A Set of Six (1990)

A six-part television comedy series produced for Channel 4; directed by Gerald Scarfe and John Stroud; starring and written by Rowland Rivron.
Spoof documentary films following the lives of sextuplets who fell out with each other on their 18th birthday.

Episode 2 features David 'Top Shot' Scrote, the most creative of the sextuplets, who is a staff photographer on the Bexhill-on-Sea Observer. Photographer David Scrote Part 1 [9:40]

The ABC Murders (1992/2018)

TV series based on the novel by Agatha Christie. Christie got to know Bexhill when her daughter Rosalind enrolled in Caledonia School for Girls in Cooden in 1928. First filmed for television in 1991 with David Suchet as Hercule Poirot, and a second adaptation 2018 where John Malkovich was cast.
A retired detective is targeted by a taunting killer who sends letters signed 'ABC', which must be decoded in order to discover the identity of the murderer.

'B' is set in Bexhill during the 1930s, with various scenes of The Colonnade and beach. The ABC Murders (1992) [59:30]

Richard III (1995)

Based on the classic Shakespearean play; directed by Richard Loncraine; starring Ian McKellen, Annette Bening, Christopher Bowen and Robert Downey Jr.
The murderous and deceitful 15th-century king is re-imagined in the setting of 1930s England.

About 40 minutes into the film a scene is shown from the top of The Colonnade, where royal family members have a garden meeting at their seaside retreat. Richard III (Trailer) [1:26]

Never Let Me Go (2010)

Based on a novel by Kazuo Ishiguro; directed by Mark Romanek; starring Keira Knightley, Carey Mulligan and Andrew Garfield.
Set in an alternative dystopian timeline where three friends become entangled in a love triangle.

In the latter half of the film De La Warr Parade, Shelter No. 2 and Berkeley Mansions (Marine Mansions) make an appearance - this filming took place over 12th and 13th May 2009. Never Let Me Go (Trailer) [2:31]

Keane: Sovereign Light Café (2012)

Alternative-rock music video; directed by Lindy Heymann; starring Keane and many local people of Bexhill.

The band members stroll along the promenade as they are surrounded by various entertainers, dancers, acrobats and bicyclists. Inspiration was drawn from where the members of Keane grew up. Keane: Sovereign Light Café [3:40]
